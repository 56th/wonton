39 /*
40  @namespace Wonton
41  The Wonton namespace houses all of the code within Wonton.
42 
43  Cells (aka zones/elements) are the highest dimension entities in a mesh
44  Nodes (aka vertices) are lowest dimension entities in a mesh
45  Faces in a 3D mesh are 2D entities, in a 2D mesh are 1D entities
46  BOUNDARY_FACE is a special type of entity that is need so that process
47  kernels can define composite vectors (see src/data_structures) on
48  exterior boundary faces of the mesh only
49 
50  Wedges are special subcell entities that are a simplicial
51  decomposition of cell. In 3D, a wedge is tetrahedron formed by one
52  point of the edge, the midpoint of the edge, the "center" of the
53  face and the "center" of the cell volume. In 2D, a wedge is a
54  triangle formed by an end-point of the edge, the mid-point of the
55  edge and the center of the cell. In 1D, wedges are lines, that are
56  formed by the endpoint of the cell and the midpoint of the
57  cell. There are two wedges associated with an edge of cell face in
58  3D.
59 
60  Corners are also subcell entities that are associated uniquely with
61  a node of a cell. Each corner is the union of all the wedges incident
62  upon that node in the cell
63 
64  Facets are the boundary entity between two wedges in adjacent
65  cells. In 3D, a facet is a triangular subface of the cell face
66  shared by two wedges in adjacent cells. In 2D, a facet is half of
67  an edge that is shared by two wedges in adjacent cells
68  */
